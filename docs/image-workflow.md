
## Crowds, Liturgies, and Implied Consent

For wide-angle photos of crowded parish settings, such as liturgies, parish picnics, parish meals, and large public gatherings, the parish may use an implied consent notice.

This does not replace good judgment. Featured photos, close-up photos, photos of minors, or photos tied to sensitive situations should still require appropriate permission.

### Public Notice

A permanent notice should be placed on the website and, if approved, periodically in the parish bulletin.

Suggested wording:

“The Catholic parishes of St. Ann, Our Lady of Perpetual Help, and Mother of Sorrows may photograph or record parish liturgies and events for parish communication, historical, and evangelization purposes. Attendance at public parish events may constitute implied consent for use in parish publications, websites, social media, and related communications. If you do not wish to be photographed or recorded, please contact the parish office.”

### Event Signage

For major parish events, place a small sign near the entrance:

“Photography and/or video may be taking place today for parish communication purposes. If you do not wish to be photographed, please notify a parish representative.”

### Approval Needed

Final wording should be approved by the pastor and, if needed, the Archdiocese.
