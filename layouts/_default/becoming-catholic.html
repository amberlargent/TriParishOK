Who Is This For?

Different paths, one Church

People come to the Catholic Church from many different places. The parish can help you understand which path fits your situation.

I have never been baptized.

Adults who have not been baptized are usually prepared for Baptism, Confirmation, and First Holy Communion through a process of prayer, formation, and discernment.

I was baptized in another Christian tradition.

Baptized Christians may be prepared to enter into full communion with the Catholic Church through profession of faith, Confirmation, and Holy Communion.

I was baptized Catholic but have been away.

Welcome home. The parish can help you return to the Sacraments and understand any next steps for Confession, Confirmation, or Holy Communion.

I want help for my child or family.

If your child needs Baptism, First Communion, Confirmation, or faith formation, the parish office can help connect you with the right preparation.

What to Expect

A faithful process of learning and conversion

1

Inquiry

Ask questions, meet someone from the parish, and share your background.

2

Formation

Learn about Jesus Christ, Scripture, prayer, the Creed, the Sacraments, the moral life, and Catholic worship.

3

Discernment

Take time to pray, ask questions, attend Mass, and prepare for the Sacraments at the proper time.

4

Reception into the Church

When properly prepared, a person may receive the Sacraments and enter into full communion with the Catholic Church.

First Step

Start with a conversation

You do not need to have everything figured out before reaching out. The first step is simply to ask questions, share where you are, and learn what the next faithful step may be.

Common Question

Can I attend Mass before becoming Catholic?

Yes. You are welcome to attend Mass. If you are not Catholic or are not prepared to receive Holy Communion, you may remain in the pew and pray during Communion.
